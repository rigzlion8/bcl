Service 1: Research & Insight Generation
Research at BCL is a bridge between lived realities and informed action.
We design mixed-methods studies combining quantitative surveys with qualitative depth. Every engagement begins with participatory co-design sessions where clients, community representatives, and implementing partners help shape the questions that matter most.
Data is collected ethically and securely using digital platforms (KoboToolbox, SurveyCTO, ODK) with real-time geo-tagged entries. We triangulate findings through thematic coding (NVivo) and statistical analysis (R, SPSS, Stata). Validation workshops bring preliminary findings back to communities for feedback, ensuring interpretations remain grounded.
Deliverables go beyond thick reports: executive summaries, visually rich infographics, policy briefs, and actionable recommendation decks.
Our research services include:
•	Baseline, midline, and endline studies
•	Needs assessments and feasibility studies
•	Impact evaluations
•	Policy and governance research
•	Market and livelihood assessments
•	Gender and social inclusion analysis
•	Learning reviews and organizational assessments
________________________________________
Service 2: Monitoring, Evaluation & Learning (MEL)
MEL at BCL turns raw data into adaptive intelligence that donors trust and programmes thrive on.
We begin every engagement by facilitating theory-of-change workshops that align indicators with both donor requirements and on-the-ground realities. Progress is tracked through digital real-time monitoring dashboards (DHIS2, Power BI, Tableau), periodic field verifications, and third-party spot-checks to maintain data integrity.
Mid-term reviews and endline assessments draw on outcome harvesting, most significant change storytelling, and OECD-DAC criteria to capture not only what changed, but why and for whom. Learning is deliberately embedded through structured after-action reflections and stakeholder dialogues.
Our MEL expertise includes:
•	MEL framework design and indicator tracking
•	Baseline, mid-term, and endline evaluations
•	Impact assessments
•	Data quality assessments
•	Real-time dashboards and reporting systems
•	Learning workshops and reflection sessions
Outcome: Partners leave with stronger adaptive capacity, credible evidence for funding renewal, and practical lessons that make future programming more effective and equitable.

Service 3: Strategic Planning & Organizational Development
Strategic planning at BCL transforms organizational ambition into a living, owned roadmap resilient to donor shifts and external shocks.
We start with diagnostic conversations – stakeholder interviews, environmental scans, and SWOT analyses – to map current realities and collective aspirations. Facilitated workshops guide teams to articulate robust theories of change, weaving in gender, youth, climate, and equity lenses. Scenario planning exercises test pathways against possible futures (funding cuts, climate events, policy changes), while prioritization tools ensure focus on high-impact interventions.
Our strategic planning services include:
•	Strategic plan development (organizational and programmatic)
•	Organizational assessments and SWOT analysis
•	Theory of Change facilitation
•	Risk analysis and scenario planning
•	Operational planning and governance strengthening
•	Institutional capacity assessments
Outcome: A dynamic strategic direction that positions organizations to remain competitive, relevant, and capable of delivering sustained impact long after the planning process ends.

Service 4: Resource Mobilization & Proposal Development
In an era of donor fatigue and intense competition, funding strategy at BCL builds financial resilience.
We begin with comprehensive donor landscape mapping – identifying aligned bilateral, multilateral, philanthropic, and private-sector opportunities, including innovative finance mechanisms. Organizational readiness is assessed, and gaps are closed so eligibility barriers are removed early.
When calls arise, we lead or co-lead the development of compelling, evidence-rich concept notes, full proposals, and budgets – always emphasizing measurable impact, value-for-money, and scalable pathways.
Our resource mobilization services include:
•	Donor landscape mapping and opportunity identification
•	Proposal development and grant writing support
•	Fundraising and partnership strategy development
•	Diversification strategies (CSR partnerships, diaspora philanthropy, unrestricted revenue)
•	Long-term donor stewardship plans
Outcome: A stronger, more diversified funding pipeline that frees organizations to focus on mission delivery rather than perpetual fundraising pressure.

Service 5: GIS Mapping & Spatial Analytics
Spatial and digital data tools at BCL turn location into leverage – bringing precision, visibility, and equity to programmes operating in remote, arid, or conflict-affected areas.
Using ArcGIS, QGIS, and Google Earth Engine, we layer satellite imagery, administrative boundaries, and community-collected data to create custom maps and hotspot analyses. Digital survey tools with built-in geo-tagging (ODK, SurveyCTO) ensure field data is accurate and instantly mappable, feeding live dashboards that programme managers can access remotely.
Our geospatial services include:
•	GIS mapping and spatial data analysis
•	Geo-tagged surveys
•	Satellite imagery analysis
•	Interactive dashboards
•	Spatial planning support
•	Programme visualization tools
Outcome: Sharper targeting, reduced wastage, more equitable reach, and compelling visual evidence that strengthens proposals and reporting for climate, health, agriculture, and livelihoods programming.

Service 6: Policy Influence & Governance Support
Policy influence and governance support at BCL uses evidence as a lever to shift systems and create enabling environments for scaled, sustainable change.
We synthesize rigorous research to pinpoint policy gaps, implementation barriers, and high-leverage entry points. Governance diagnostics assess institutional structures, decision-making flows, financial management, and capacity constraints.
From diagnostics, we co-create advocacy strategies – stakeholder power maps, influence pathways, messaging frameworks, and coalition-building plans – designed to move key decision-makers. Economic and financial modelling tests reform options, quantifying costs, benefits, and returns.
Our policy and governance services include:
•	Policy reviews and governance assessments
•	Advocacy strategy development
•	Feasibility studies and reform roadmaps
•	Multi-stakeholder dialogue facilitation
•	Capacity building for governance institutions
Outcome: More responsive systems, clearer pathways to influence, and organizations better equipped to shape the environments they operate in.

Digital transformation and AI integration at BCL is handled with deliberate responsibility – technology amplifies human insight and community voice, never replaces it.
We begin with a participatory digital maturity assessment to map current systems, skill levels, pain points, and organizational ambitions. Solutions are chosen thoughtfully: low/no-code platforms and ethical AI applications that integrate seamlessly without demanding heavy infrastructure.
Our digital services include:
•	AI-assisted analytics and reporting
•	Automated MEL dashboards
•	Real-time data visualization
•	Workflow automation
•	Predictive analytics for early risk detection
•	Ethical AI advisory (bias audits, data privacy safeguards)
•	Digital literacy and responsible AI training
We align with the African Union's Continental AI Strategy and emerging data sovereignty principles, ensuring all implementations remain inclusive, ethical, and context-sensitive.
Outcome: Faster insight cycles, greater efficiency, stronger donor competitiveness, and organizations that are digitally mature and inclusive – ready for a funding landscape that increasingly rewards responsible innovation.

We provide end-to-end support from proposal development and readiness assessments to implementation support and rigorous MEL. Geospatial and AI-enhanced tools enable precise vulnerability mapping, early-warning systems, and real-time tracking of adaptation outcomes.
Our climate services include:
•	Climate vulnerability assessments
•	Climate adaptation strategy development
•	Climate finance proposal development (GCF, AfDB Adaptation Fund, readiness windows)
•	NDC alignment support
•	Climate MEL systems (GIS- and AI-enhanced)
•	Capacity building for NDAs, ministries, and NGOs
•	Spatial modelling and early-warning systems

Service 9: Agriculture & Food Security
Our Agriculture & Food Security service focuses on sustainable farming systems that enhance food sovereignty and resilience against climate shocks.
We conduct value chain analyses, promote climate-smart practices, and integrate geospatial tools to map fertile zones, risks, and priority intervention areas. Policy advocacy, market access, and smallholder inclusion are embedded to ensure equitable benefits for women and youth farmers.
Outcome: Clients – from NGOs to governments – secure funding, reduce food insecurity, foster economic growth, and align with continental food sovereignty goals.

Our Health & WASH service delivers integrated solutions for water, sanitation, hygiene, and health equity in vulnerable communities.
We assess needs through participatory research, design climate-resilient WASH infrastructure with GIS precision, and evaluate programmes for disease prevention, nutrition outcomes, and overall wellbeing. Approaches are designed for urban, peri-urban, and rural settings to ensure broad, equitable reach.
Outcome: Donor-compliant MEL, AI-enhanced monitoring, and resilient strategies that attract health and adaptation funding and partnerships.
